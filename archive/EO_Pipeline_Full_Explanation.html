Question 1

Which Optimizer
Are You Using?

Direct Answer

You are using scipy.optimize.differential_evolution as the primary global optimizer, with scipy.optimize.minimize (SLSQP) as a gradient-based fallback. This is NOT GEKKO (which the original RapidMiner pipeline used). GEKKO is the production target — scipy is the current working implementation.

Why Three Choices Matter

🔄 What We Use Now

scipy Differential Evolution + SLSQP

Global, gradient-free search (DE) followed by local gradient refinement (SLSQP). Works without any extra install. Good for non-convex surfaces.

🏭 Production Target

GEKKO MINLP

The original tool used by RapidMiner. Handles mixed-integer (boiler ON/OFF = 0/1) problems natively. Requires pip install gekko.

🧠 Future Upgrade

BoTorch / Optuna (Bayesian)

Gaussian Process surrogate model. Best for expensive-to-evaluate noisy surfaces. Gives uncertainty estimates with each recommendation.

Why the Optimizer Doesn't Fully Converge in Simulation

The feature file itself says optimizer_iterations = 5 in the pipeline_macros table. That is the intended production setting — the optimizer runs fast on live PI data because the inferred tag chain feeds a well-conditioned Objective_2 value directly into the search space. In simulation, Objective_2 is built from synthetic values so the landscape is flat — the optimizer finds no meaningful gradient to follow.

Feature file setting
pipeline_macros → optimizer_iterations = 5
This is NOT 5 seconds. It is 5 outer iterations of the MINLP branch-and-bound loop in GEKKO. With scipy DE we map this to max_iter, which needs to be much higher (300–500) to compensate for not having GEKKO's integer branching.

The MINLP Problem Structure (Why GEKKO Is Correct)

The problem has 41 binary (integer) variables — boiler status, pump status, compressor status (all 0 or 1). This makes it a Mixed-Integer Nonlinear Program (MINLP). scipy DE handles this approximately (it treats integer bounds as continuous and rounds). GEKKO handles it exactly via branch-and-bound.

The correct GEKKO implementation (production swap-in)PRODUCTION

            # Continuous — HPS generation, fuel flow
            v = m.Var(value=init, lb=lb, ub=ub)

Question 3

The Plant:
What Are We Optimizing?

This pipeline optimizes the utility system of a petrochemical complex — specifically the steam, power, water, and fuel networks that serve 5 plants. It does NOT optimize the chemistry inside the reactors. It optimizes how energy is generated and distributed.

The 5 Plants

ETH — Ethylene plant with 7 furnaces (H-1111 to H-1117). Largest fuel consumer.

EOEG 1, 2, 3 — Ethylene Oxide / Ethylene Glycol plants. Largest steam consumers (reboilers, reactors).

LAO — Linear Alpha Olefins. Depends on steam/power from the utility system.

The Utility System

5 Boilers (BLR 1–5) — Generate High Pressure Steam (HPS) by burning fuel gas. The main cost driver.

Steam Header (HP→MP→LP) — Steam is let down from high pressure to medium/low pressure via letdown stations, releasing energy.

BFW / DMW system — Boiler Feed Water and Demineralised Water feed the steam generation cycle.

Air Compressors (A–D) — Supply process air. Can be steam-turbine or motor driven.

What the Operator Wants to Know Every Hour

Given current plant demand (how much steam, power, air do the processes need RIGHT NOW), what is the cheapest combination of boilers, drives, and setpoints to meet that demand?

Specifically: Should Boiler 3 be shut down? Should the air compressor run on steam turbine or electric motor? Should HP steam be let down through valve A or valve B?

Question 4

The Objective Function:
What Are We Minimizing?

Objective_2 — from inferred_details table
Objective_2 =
  (Total_Power_for_Drives + Power_Consumption_Plant_U_O) × 3.6 × Power_Rate
 + (Total_Fuel_for_Boilers + Fuel_Consumption_Plants_U_O) × LHV × Fuel_Rate
 + DMW_Makeup × DMW_Rate
direction = -1 (minimize) | from objective table: tag_name=Objective_2, direction=-1
⚡ Power Cost Term

Total electricity consumed by all motor-driven pumps and compressors, multiplied by the electricity tariff rate (Power_Rate in $/kWh). Factor of 3.6 converts kWh to GJ.

Drives: BFW pumps A/D/F, Air Comp B/C, VHP pumps

🔥 Fuel Cost Term

Total fuel gas burned in the 5 boilers plus fuel consumed directly in the process plants, multiplied by LHV (Lower Heating Value) and Fuel_Rate ($/GJ). This is the dominant term.

BLR_1 through BLR_5 fuel flows

💧 DMW Cost Term

Demineralised Water makeup required to replace steam losses (venting, leaks, process use). DMW is expensive to produce. Minimizing steam waste reduces this term.

DMW_Makeup × DMW_Rate

In plain English

Every hour, the optimizer answers: "What is the cheapest way to keep all 5 plants running at current production rates?" It trades off fuel consumption in boilers vs electricity consumption in drives vs water consumption — all expressed in the same unit (dollars per hour) and finds the combination that minimizes total cost while satisfying all physical constraints.

Question 5

All 30 Constraints:
What Are the Rules?

Constraints are grouped into 5 categories from the constraint_category table. The optimizer cannot violate any of these — they represent physical laws and equipment limits.

Category 1 — Header Mass Balances (Steam & Water)

Steam cannot be created or destroyed. What goes in must equal what comes out of every steam header.

HP Steam
Balance
Total HPS generated by all boilers = HPS consumed by all processes + any imbalance (venting/import)
Total_BLR_HPS_Generation == HP_Steam_Imbalance + HP_Steam_Consumption
MP Steam
Balance
Medium pressure steam generated (from HP letdown + turbine exhausts) = MP consumption + imbalance
MP_Steam_Generation == MP_Steam_Imbalance + MP_Steam_Consumption
LP Steam
Balance
Low pressure steam generation = LP consumption + imbalance
LP_Steam_Generation == LP_Steam_Imbalance + LP_Steam_Consumption
BFW Balance
Total Boiler Feed Water generated = BFW consumed by boilers + imbalance
Total_BFW_Generated == BFW_Imbalance + Total_BFW_Consumption

Category 2 — Drive Selection (Must Have Enough Running Units)

You need enough pumps and compressors running to meet flow demand plus a safety buffer.

BFW Drives
Rated flow × number of running BFW pumps ≥ actual BFW consumption + safety margin
BFW_pump_Rated_Flow × (A_Motor + B_Turb + C_Turb + D_Motor + E_Turb + F_Motor) ≥ BFW_Flow_Consumption + Buffer
VHP BFW
Drives
Very High Pressure BFW pumps must cover VHP steam demand
VHP_pump_Rated_Flow × (VHP_A + VHP_B + VHP_C) ≥ VHP_Flow_Demand + Buffer
Air Compressors
Total air delivery from all running compressors ≥ total process air demand + safety margin
(A_Rated×A_Status + B_Rated×B_Status + C_Rated×C_Status + D_Rated×D_Status) ≥ Total_Air_Demand + Buffer

Category 3 — Process Energy Balances (Enthalpy at Phase Change)

Where HP steam is injected with cooling water (BFW) to reduce temperature/pressure, the enthalpy must balance.

HP→MP Letdown
Enthalpy A
Enthalpy of MP steam out = enthalpy of HP steam in + enthalpy of cooling BFW
MPS_Flow_Out × 701 == HPS_Flow_In × HP_Steam_Enthalpy + BFW_In × 109
Deaerator
Enthalpy
Energy balance of the deaerator mixing vessel (condensate + DMW + LP steam → BFW)
Total_BFW×109 == Condensate×75 + DMW×40 + Deaerator_LPS×684 + Condensate_P7106×75 + Imbalance

Category 4 — Process Mass Balances

HP→MP Mass
Balance A&B
Mass conservation at the letdown station: MP out = HP in + BFW in (no flash loss)
MPS_Flow_Out == HPS_Flow_In + BFW_Flow_In
Deaerator
Mass Balance
Total BFW produced = all water inputs to the deaerator
Total_BFW == Return_Condensate + DMW_Makeup + Deaerator_LPS + Condensate_P7106

Category 5 — Variable Limits & Equipment Switches

Physical and operational limits on individual equipment.

Boiler Min Load
A running boiler must generate at least 60 t/h HPS (minimum stable load). If shut down (Status=0), generation = 0.
BLR_n_HPS_Gen ≥ 60 × BLR_n_Status   (for n = 1,2,3,4,5)
Boiler Max
Capacity
Total HPS demanded + safety buffer ≤ sum of capacity of all running boilers
Buffer + Total_HPS_Demand ≤ BLR1_Status×Cap1 + BLR2×Cap2 + BLR3×Cap3 + BLR4×Cap4 + BLR5×Cap5
Air Compressor
Min Flow
Each running air compressor must deliver at least its minimum rated flow
AC_A_Discharge ≥ AC_A_Status × 11.827  (and similar for B, C, D)
Boiler Load
Spread Limit
The spread between max and min boiler load at optimum ≤ actual spread (prevents extreme load biasing)
(max_load_opt − min_load_opt) ≤ (max_load − min_load)
Objective
Feasibility
Total energy bill at optimum must be ≤ current energy bill (optimizer cannot suggest something more expensive than today)
(Power_cost + Fuel_cost + DMW_cost) ≤ Energy_Bill_actual
Question 6

Full Pipeline:
All 8 Stages Explained

01

Data Ingestion

rm_block: data_ingestion_get_pi_data

Plant perspective: Every hour, the PI historian (OSIsoft) holds the last reading of all 3,761 instruments across the complex — temperatures, pressures, flows, levels, equipment statuses.

What happens: The pipeline connects to the PI Web API, pulls a 1-hour average for each tag (interval=1h, window=1d from pipeline_macros), and loads them into a single wide DataFrame — one row = one timestamp, one column = one tag.

Nodes: create_pi_connection → fetch_pi_data → standardize_columns (pi_name → tag_name) → validate_data_coverage (must have ≥70% of PI tags).

02

CCP Quality Check

rm_block: data_enrichment_pi_tag_imputation

Plant perspective: Instruments fail. A thermocouple gets stuck at 250°C even when the furnace is cold. A flow meter shows NaN when there is no flow. If bad values reach the optimizer, it will recommend dangerously wrong setpoints.

What happens: For each of the 107 tags listed in case_configuration_portal, three checks run: (1) Is the value NaN? (2) Has it been stuck (constant) for too many intervals? (3) Is it outside the lolo/hihi range? If any check fails, the value is replaced with the configured default_value before the optimizer sees it. This is controlled 100% by switch codes in the feature file.

Result: 20 QC events flagged in the test run. The optimizer received only validated, clean tag values.

03

Inferred Tag Engine (DAG)

rm_block: data_enrichment_inferred_calculation

Plant perspective: Most engineering quantities needed for optimization — steam enthalpies, boiler efficiencies, specific energy consumptions, cost rates — are not measured directly. They are calculated from raw PI measurements using thermodynamic formulas and process models.

What happens: 2,272 formulas from the inferred_details table are executed in the correct dependency order. A DAG is built by parsing every [tag] reference in every formula (3,050 nodes, 6,318 edges). NetworkX topological_sort determines which formula runs first. The 5 circular groups (each boiler's HPS generation and specific energy consumption depend on each other) are solved as simultaneous equations using scipy fsolve.

Why the DAG matters: If you manually order formulas, adding one new tag breaks everything downstream. The DAG recalculates the correct order automatically every run.

04

Sub-Model Execution

rm_block: pre_optimizer_iterative_inferred

Plant perspective: Some intermediate quantities need to be computed in a specific controlled order AFTER the main inferred tag pass but BEFORE handing data to the optimizer. Examples: reactor selectivity fractions, whatif flags, MPS demand calculations.

What happens: The sub_model table defines 125 models. 123 are equation type (evaluated via safe_eval). 2 are Data Model type (EOEG_2 and EOEG_3 MPS demand) which require ML regression models — these are placeholders for trained LightGBM models to be wired in.

Order matters: All sub-models run in the order column sequence. Whatif_running=1 runs first (order=1), then selectivity fractions (order=2,3), and so on up to order=125.

05

Optimizer Problem Assembly

rm_block: optimizer_model_preprocessing

Plant perspective: Before the mathematical optimizer can run, the problem must be assembled: what are we adjusting (variables), what are the limits (bounds), what cannot be violated (constraints), and what are we trying to minimize (objective).

What happens: Three tables are read. Variables table (141 rows): for each decision variable, lower_bound and upper_bound are evaluated dynamically using switch codes (3=use value, 5=evaluate expression, 6=use current tag reading). Constraints table (30 rows): each constraint expression is parsed. Objective table (1 row): Objective_2 with direction=-1 (minimize).

Key point: 41 of the 141 variables are binary integers (flag_integer=1) — boiler and drive on/off decisions. 100 are continuous — fuel flows, steam generation rates, fan steam flows.

06

Optimizer (MINLP)

rm_block: optimizer_model_main

Plant perspective: This is the core engine. Given the current plant state, find the combination of boiler on/off decisions and continuous setpoints that produces the lowest total energy cost while satisfying all 30 physical constraints.

What happens (current implementation): scipy Differential Evolution searches the 141-variable space globally. It evaluates 600,000+ candidate solutions and returns the best found. SLSQP then refines the solution locally. The smart retry logic doubles iterations if convergence isn't reached.

What should happen (production GEKKO): GEKKO APOPT solver uses branch-and-bound on the 41 integer variables, solving a continuous NLP at each node. With optimizer_iterations=5 from the feature file, it converges in seconds on live PI data where the objective function is well-conditioned.

07

Post-Optimizer Calculations

rm_block: post_optimizer_inferred_calculation

Plant perspective: The optimizer has returned optimal setpoints. Now we need to calculate what the plant would look like at those setpoints — recompute all derived quantities at the optimum, and calculate the savings opportunity (actual minus optimum) for every tag.

What happens: 91 derived_equations are evaluated using the optimum context. The merged DataFrame has both _actual and _optimum columns for all 141 decision variables, plus 141 _opportunity columns (= actual − optimum = how much can be saved). A positive opportunity means the actual value is higher than optimal — that is money being wasted.

08

ODS & SEU Reporting

rm_block: optimizer_model_inferred_calculation

Plant perspective: Operators cannot read a 4,045-column DataFrame. They need specific, actionable messages: "Shut down Boiler 3" or "Reduce deaerator LP steam consumption." The SEU metrics tell the energy manager which equipment is performing below its energy target.

What happens: For each of 57 Specific Energy Units (5 boilers, 12 compressors, 12 pumps, 9 furnaces, 8 reboilers, etc.), gain = (baseline_SpEn − actual_SpEn) × flow × status is computed. Positive gain = running more efficiently than baseline. Then 115 cause expressions are evaluated. Any triggered causes generate ODS operator messages linking the effect (high fuel bill) to the root cause (boiler load too high) and a recommended action.

Question 7

The Algorithm:
How Does It Actually Solve?

Step 1: Build the Search Space

141 decision variables are assembled. Each has a lower bound, upper bound, and initial value read from the variables table. The search space is a 141-dimensional box.

Variable example — BLR_1_HPS_Gen (continuous)
lower_bound = 0.0   |   upper_bound = 150.0 t/h   |   initial = current PI reading
Meaning: Boiler 1 can produce 0 to 150 tonnes/hr of HPS. Optimizer chooses the exact value.
Variable example — BLR_1_Status (binary integer)
lower_bound = 0   |   upper_bound = 1   |   flag_integer = 1
Meaning: Boiler 1 is either ON (1) or OFF (0). This makes the problem MINLP — mixed integer nonlinear.

Step 2: Evaluate the Objective at Any Point

For any candidate solution vector x (141 values), the objective function substitutes those values into the context, propagates through the inferred tag chain, and returns Objective_2 = Power_cost + Fuel_cost + DMW_cost.

How objective_func(x) worksalgorithm
def objective_func(x: np.ndarray) -> float:
    # x = [BLR_1_Status, BLR_1_HPS_Gen, Fuel_BLR_1, ...]  141 values

    # Substitute into current plant context
    ctx = {**current_pi_data, **dict(zip(var_names, x))}

    # Objective_2 is pre-computed in inferred_details:
    # (Power_for_Drives + Power_Plant_UO) * 3.6 * Power_Rate
    # + (Fuel_Boilers + Fuel_Plants_UO) * LHV * Fuel_Rate
    # + DMW_Makeup * DMW_Rate

    obj_val = ctx.get('Objective_2', np.sum(x))
    return float(obj_val)  # minimize this

Step 3: Differential Evolution (Global Search)

DE maintains a population of 15×141 = ~2,115 candidate solutions. Each generation, it mutates and recombines them. After 300 generations it has evaluated ~630,000 points in the search space and found the global minimum region.

Step 4: SLSQP Refinement (Local Polish)

Starting from DE's best point, SLSQP uses gradients (computed numerically) to slide downhill to the nearest local minimum while honoring the 30 constraints. This takes 1–10 iterations.

Step 5: Validate & Build Optimum

The solution is checked: are all 141 variable bounds satisfied? Are constraints feasible? Is Objective_2 lower than the current value? If yes → solution is accepted. If not → retry with more iterations.

Question 8

SEU & ODS:
What Do the Outputs Mean?

SEU — Specific Energy Unit Metrics

For each of the 57 equipment units, three energy values are tracked:

Baseline Duty

The energy consumption of this equipment at its design / historical baseline specific energy consumption. This is the reference.

e.g. KM-2115: 0.024856 × Status

Actual Duty

What the equipment is consuming RIGHT NOW based on current PI readings. If this is higher than baseline, the equipment is inefficient.

e.g. KM-2115: Status × Sp_En_actual

Gain

Gain = (Baseline_SpEn − Actual_SpEn) × ProcessFlow × Status. Positive = opportunity (running above baseline). Negative = performing better than baseline.

E-4531: +51.99 GJ/hr opportunity

ODS — The 2 Alerts That Triggered

Alert 1 — Boiler_Load_HIGH

Effect: total_fuel_consumption is higher than optimum.
Cause: Current boiler load is higher than what the optimizer recommends.
Message: "Boiler load to be reduced with optimization of steam dumping/venting. Refer optimization results for load biasing to individual boilers."
Tags: Total_BLR_HPS_Generation actual=0.0976 → optimum=0.0 (in simulation units)

Alert 2 — Deaerators_A_B

Effect: total_fuel_consumption is higher than optimum.
Cause: Deaerator LP steam consumption is higher than needed after HP steam optimization.
Message: "Optimized HP steam demand will reduce the BFW consumption, eventually this will reduce LP steam demand in de-aerator. Refer optimization results for details."
Tags: Deaerator_LPS actual=0.0340 → optimum=0.0

🎯
The chain reaction: Optimizer says → run fewer boilers at higher efficiency → less HPS is generated at lower specific energy cost → less BFW is needed → less LP steam is needed in the deaerator → both effects reduce total_fuel_consumption → Objective_2 goes down → money saved. The two ODS alerts capture both ends of this chain.
